Altdorf

Lets explore the data, and some descriptive statistics, its distribution, the time series etc. I will start of with only the station Altdorf as a placeholder for all individual stations.

HOSPITALIZATIONS

How are hospitalizations distributed over the year? Hospitalisations rates are highest in winter and lowest in summer. This is the case for men, women and all age groups. However, divided by diseases, this pattern seems to be driven by cardiovascular and respiratory diseases.

How do yearly hospitalization develop over time? Hospitalizations are increasing. There are rapid increases in 2008 and 2018. Men show higher hospitalization rates. Although the trend overall and in men could be due to demographics.

What are the yearly hospitalizations per age and disease? Here again, we see the rising trend which is strongest in the age group 15-64. 0-14 year olds even have a slight decreasing trend while all other age group slightly increase in hospitalizations over time. Divided by disease groups, cardiovascular diseases show the steepest increase with its curve resembling the overall hospitalization trend quite well. All other disease show a small trend but are hard to distinguish from another. Again, the interpretation of the observed trends is limited by the unknown demographic development.

FOEHN

Is there a trend in annual foehn or seasonality? We have a clear seasonal trend in foehn with a its peak in spring and intermediate levels in late autumn and winter. In summer there is only litte foehn on average per day. Over the observation period, foehn seems to decrease in its total occurence time per year. From the distribution (bottom left) it can be concluded, that on theoverarching majority of the days we have no foehn. Foehn frequency is antiproporional to its intensity, with a slight peak at 288 foehn score as this is the highest achievable for a day.

TEMPERATURE

Whats the annual path of temperature in Altdorf? Do we see an increasing trend of temperature? As expected, it is warmest in summer with the highest monthly average in July just below 20 degrees C and coldest in winter just above 0 degrees. The yearly temperature trend has an increasing tendency. We see the hot years of 2003 and 2018 very clearly in Altdorf.

How is temperature distributed? Temperature has values between -10 and 30 degrees C average daily temperature. Temperature has no gaussian distribution but has in the median between the extremes a decrease in counts. 75% of all mean daily temperature values are between 5 and 15 degrees C.

CORRELATIONS

Do we have obvious correltaions in the dataset? As already determined from the plots above, we have the greatest corelation with all hospitalizations from 15-64 year-olds and from cardiovascular diseases. In this a age group, there are more men than women, however, more men are also hospitalized than women. From all disease groups, the largest correlation was found with hospitalizations due to mental illnesses. 65-74 year-olds correlate most with cardiovascular admission and equally with men and women. Above 85 year-olds also correlate more with cvd but this time also with women (could be due to longer lifespan of women and harvesting in below 65 year-olds). Interestingly, below 15 year-olds and temperature and foehn all show no correlations with any other subgroup.
